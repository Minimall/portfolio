Ryanair:Check-in experience redesign

Role UX Designer (check-in flow) Location Dublin, Ireland (in-house) Link ryanair.com 2014

Ryanair is Europe’s favorite airline, carrying over 130m customers yearly from 34 countries and delivering Europe’s No.1 on-time performance, and 32-year safety record.

At some moment they had gotten a bit off-track with their website & mobile apps and I was invited to fix this as part of EPAM design & development team.

I worked with the Ryanair team in-house at Dublin for 4 months as UX designer.

My role

Research

I collaborated with the Head of Digital Experience at Ryanair on defining business goals of redesign & identifying key insights from company analytics. I also crawled through user feedback from Ryanair’s support & public sources and discovered some hidden questions & problems.

Prototyping

I built multiple interactive prototypes, including micro interactions & states for an efficient user testing and for better communication inside our team.

Design

I designed every aspect of the booking & check-in flows on the Ryanair website, from low-fi prototypes to development-ready assets, including animations.

Low-cost airlines don’t make money on tickets.

They make it on cross-sales while booking & check-in.

Discover phase

The first step was to determine important use cases that drive client’s KPIs.

Through day-to-day interactions with stakeholders and current users, I collected information about booking & check-in process to understand pricing model, marketing patterns and special insights of an industry leading low-cost airline.

Research also contained a thorough analysis of competitive products. Consequently, I figured out a clear and overall challenge for this task:

Challenge

To make check-in experience highlight relevant & affordable travel extras, while increasing business KPIs, and ideally increasing user engagement.

Personalized check-in

During the discovery phase, one of the low-level UX problems to be solved was revealed: users were distracted by cross-sales & reported negative feedback about special Ryanair services, that was offered during online check-in.

After many initial explorations (a few represented below), I realized that the travel extras suggestions are a part of often recurrent travel routines. They appear occasionally in your ‘Travel To-do’ list, like «rent a car», «add ski equipment to booking», «find a cheap and cozy hotel» and others.

I went deeper into the idea of personalized check-in process with relevant service suggestions. By reorganizing typical steps of check-in into a seamless story, I created a flow, that user perceives as a friendly assistant, which makes check-in more personal & quicker while following requirements of a business.

Visual design

While diving into visual details I kept in mind the overall look and feel of the brand: minimalistic and trustworthy.

Therefore I tried to stick with a simple theme and a familiar navigation followed by global website guidelines. After all, it’s meant to be read by the diverse audience including different ages, cultures & health problems.

 

This case will be updated later, with more updates on visual design and animation.

At the end, I learned how to connect business expectations and user needs into a beautiful and working solution.
